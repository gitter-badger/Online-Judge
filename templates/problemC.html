Problem : The Algorithmic Station | Time limit: 0.01secs | Memory: 800B | Submit code

Last december Team Illustrious went to Kerala. They boarded the train (Number of compartments in train is N) at 'The Algorithmic Railway Station'.
The speciality of the railway station is that each time any two numbers (a,b<=1,000,000) are announced for K times.
On each announcement of two numbers a and b, only one person boards each compartment from compartment a to compartment b (inclusive of a,b).
Suppose, if the announced numbers are 10 and 12, one person boards in compartment 10, one in compartment 11, one in 12.
After t announcements, Team Illustrious wants to know the number of passengers in the middle compartment of the train when train's compartments are sorted in increasing order of the passengers.

Constraints:
N<=10^6 and N is odd.
K<=25,000
a<=10^6 and b<=10^6


Input:
First line of each testcase contains N,K seperated by a single space. Follows K lines, each line containing two numbers a,b.

Output:
For each test case print the number of passengers from the first compartment to the middle compartment(inclusive of first and middle compartments) and the number of passengers in all the compartments of the train in a line seperated by a single space.

Sample Input:

3
7 4
5 5
2 4
4 6
3 5

Sample Output:
3 10
